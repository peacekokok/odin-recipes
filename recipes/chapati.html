Kenyan Chapati RecipeS

Description

Chapati is a widely cooked dish with origins from India. Through the Arab Trade, chapati was brought to Eastern Africa. I like many Kenyans have grown up with a sweeter version of Chapati than the one available in India. The recipe below will outline the Kenyan version of chapati.

Ingredients

  • Water
  • 4 cups All Purpose Flour and 1 cup for rolling
  • 1 Tablespoon Oil
  • 4 table spoons Sugar
  • 2 teaspoons Salt

Steps

  1. Gather ingredients
  2. Mix the all purpose flour with sugar and salt
  3. Slowly add to the oil to the mixture
  4. Add a cup of warm water
  5. Keep molding dough until you get a solid but bouncy texture
  6. Once the dough is solid place it in an oiled large bowl and cover with warm wet towel
  7. Let sit for 1 hour
  8. Roll dough into circle and put oil on the surface
  9. Create smaller balls from the big circle
  10. Let sit for 15 minutes
  11. Begin to roll dough into circles
  12. Heat up pan
  13. Place rolled chapati dough on the pan
  14. Look as bubbles form on the dough and then flip the chapati
  15. Once both sides have a golden brown tone to them take the chapati off the heat
  16. Repeat this until all dough is finished
